Menorca, declared a Biosphere Reserve by the UNESCO because of its environmental diversity and landscapes, offers a wide range of alternatives to the visitor.

There is a wide offer of sporting activities, such us windsurfing, scuba diving, horse-riding, cycling, tennis, golf and even cricket.

Special attention should be paid to its prehistoric settlements and monuments spread throughout the island territory.

There are several museums worth seeing, such as the Museum of Nature in Ferreries, the municipal museum Bastió de Sa Font in Ciutadella, or the Menorca and Hernández-Sanz museums in Mahon.

The main musical events of the year are: Weeks of opera in the Teatre Principal of Maó, the oldest opera theater in Spain; the Davidic Capella concerts in Ciutadella, and the summer music festivals in Mahon and Ciutadella. Also, jazz concerts are booming and have joined the musical offerings of the island.

For nature lovers, the biggest attraction of the island is without doubt the beauty and richness of its natural environment, which includes most of the ecosystems in the Mediterranean. This is why it has been declared by UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Menorca is also an intensely humanized territory with a rich rural tradition.
The best way to discover and enjoy the treasures of the island is crossing it on foot or bicycle through the Cami de Cavalls, an ancient route of 185 km that runs along the coast and that can be undertaken in Arenal d'en Castell.
